The story behind the song · Part 1

The Mountain

This song is my story. It's about the moment a blow to the head changed everything I knew: how I thought, how I moved through the world, even how I remembered who I was. After my traumatic brain injury (TBI), I felt like an empty shell in the dark — watching my life come apart while memory slipped away like sand through my hands. Faces I'd known for years became hard to recognize. Simple things I once leaned on were suddenly gone. Days got heavy, nights got long, and frustration rolled through me like a storm.

My brothers told me I didn't seem the same. That was the moment I knew I couldn't hide the pain anymore. I was scared of what was drifting away, trying desperately to hold on to yesterday while my present kept slipping. I knew I needed help to survive, reaching for answers in the night, but I didn't know where to turn.

The "mountain" in this song is that period in my life — the place where I almost let everything fall away: my work, my place, the life I'd built. In a way, the man who climbed that mountain never made it through. What came back down wore his face, but stronger and more true.

"I came back changed, but I came back whole.
I still carry scars, but I found my soul again."

"The one who went to the mountain was lost to it — what returned wore his face, but not his soul." — BajaTroy

The story behind the song · Part 2

The Way Back

The turning point came when help finally arrived. With the support of a caring association and people who refused to let me disappear, the pieces started coming back. I finally got the help I had been asking for, and I could feel my life return once more. That support didn't just give me stability; it gave me momentum. It gave me back enough of myself to try creating again.

That's where AI comes in. Songwriting had always been part of who I was, but after my TBI, the process felt impossibly hard. Planning lyrics, tracking structure, remembering musical ideas — it all became overwhelming. AI tools became my assistants: helping me organize thoughts, sketch lyrics, refine melodies, and map out song structures without the mental load that once stopped me.

"AI didn't replace my creativity; it supported it.
It let me write again, even when my brain was still healing."

This song is the result: a personal testimony of survival, recovery, and rediscovery. It's also part of my larger mission — to show people with TBI and other disabilities that they can still create, write, and express themselves, even if the process has to look different than it did before. Through tutorials and examples like this, I want to show how AI-assisted songwriting can be a realistic, doable path forward, especially when traditional methods feel too heavy or too complicated.

And now I know I'm not alone. I made my way back home — not to the exact life I had before, but to a steady ground where I can stand, create, and live with purpose again.

"If you're on that mountain, trying to hold on, there is help.
There is a way back. And with the right tools and support,
you can come back changed, but whole."

Phase 1 · Foundation & Storytelling

Pick your lane, write your heart

1 · Select your genre & taste profile

Identify the musical lane that resonates with your identity or taste.

FolkOutlaw CountrySpaghetti WesternYOUR genre here

2 · Write the core story — the "heart" of the song

Draft a narrative prose block: a personal experience, a family legacy, or a fictional journey. Write freely — no rhyme, no structure, no rules yet.

For "Back From The Mountain," the heart was the testimony itself — the blow to the head, the brothers' warning, the mountain, the way back. The story you just read on slides 2 and 3 became the song.

In Troy's words

"I write a story first. Whether it's my background, true story or fake story, my ethnic background and my story of my family I chose. And then I pick the style, and then I write a story."

Phase 2 · AI Lyric Construction

The Thesaurus Method

1 · Structural framework

Map your story into song components — verses, a repeating chorus, an outro. "Back From The Mountain" became: Verse 1 (the injury) → Verse 2 (the dark days) → Chorus (the mountain) → Verse 3 (the help) → Chorus → Outro (home).

2 · AI vocabulary support

Feed your prose to an AI tool. Ask for rhyming pairs, rhythmic phrases, and emotional synonyms that fit your melody — while keeping your original voice.

CHARGED LANGUAGE AMPLIFIES THE EMOTIONAL FEELINGS TO THE LISTENER.

ANYONE CAN THROW WORDS INTO AI and it will write a song, but remember that is not your song. Take pride in creating your story and sharing your emotional creativity.

Now watch how the testimony became lyrics:

"Days got heavy, nights got long"→"Days got heavy, nights got long"
"stronger and more true… came back whole… found my soul"→rhyme chain: through / true / whole / soul
"help was found… steady ground"→rhyme pair: found / ground
"not alone… made my way back home"→rhyme pair: alone / home
In Troy's words

"With AI, I narrow it down and I use AI like a thesaurus. Sometimes I pick words from my original story to complement the rhyming and the melody."

Phase 4 · Generate & Edit

The Creative Loop

Input your lyrics and style tags into a generative music platform (Suno, Udio). Listen. Then go back and tweak — as many times as it takes.

Generate→ Listen→ Tweak words & style→ Re-generate↺

Repeat until the track perfectly captures your vision.

In Troy's words

"It'll spit it out, and you can go in there and edit it and change things around. You just keep creating, go back, edit, keep creating — until you get the sound that you like."
